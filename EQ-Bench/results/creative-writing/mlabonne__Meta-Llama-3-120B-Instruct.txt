Historical Fiction: Write a scene from a story set during the height of the Roman Empire, focusing on a slice of a day in the life of a gladiator. Do not write a combat scene. Use sensory details to capture the sights, sounds, and smells of ancient Rome. Explore the gladiator's thoughts and emotions. I woke before the sun, my skin slick with sweat, my mouth dry as the African sand that covered the training grounds. The air was heavy with the stench of human waste and yesterday's blood, a noxious cloud that clung to me like a shroud. I threw off the thin blanket and sat up, my back against the cold stone wall of my cell. Slaves scurried back and forth, carrying steaming jugs of wine and platters of bread, while the lanista, our grizzled old trainer, bellowed orders at the top of his lungs. I fell in line behind the others, my stomach growling with hunger, and waited my turn at the trough.

The food was the same as always: congealed gruel made from who-knew-what grain, maybe some scraps of old meat if we were lucky. I ate quickly, mechanically, my mind elsewhere. I thought of my family, of the farm we'd left behind in Dacia, of the fields of golden wheat that stretched as far as the eye could see. I thought of my sister, of her bright smile and her laughter, of the way she'd curl up in my lap at night and fall asleep with her head on my chest.

I pushed the thoughts away, forced myself to focus on the present. Today was not a day for sentimentality. Today was a day for survival.

After breakfast, we filed out into the training grounds, our sandals scuffing against the dirt. The sun was up now, a burning disk of fire that beat down on us like a flail. I squinted against the glare, my eyes watering, and made my way to my corner of the arena.

My opponent for the day was already waiting for me, a hulking brute of a Thracian with arms as thick as tree trunks and a face like a side of raw meat. He glared at me with a look that said he'd like to gut me on the spot, and I smiled back at him, my heart pounding in my chest.

"Salve, Marcus," I said, my voice light, my hand extended in greeting.

The Thracian grunted something in his own language, but I just shrugged and turned away. I didn't need to understand him to know what he was thinking.

We sparred for what felt like hours, our wooden swords clashing, our bodies slick with sweat. The sun beat down on us, relentless, until I felt like I was fighting not just my opponent, but the very gods themselves.

Finally, the lanista called a halt, and we stumbled, gasping, out of the arena. I collapsed onto the nearest bench, my chest heaving, my muscles screaming in protest. One of the slaves, a spindly little Greek boy with big brown eyes, scurried over with a jug of posca, the vinegary wine that passed for water in this dump.

"Domine," he whispered, his voice trembling, as he poured a cupful over my head.

I closed my eyes, let the cool liquid wash over me, and felt, for a moment, a fleeting sense of peace. I opened my eyes to find the lanista standing over me, his face a map of scars and wrinkles.

"You fight like a girl, Dacian," he snarled, his breath reeking of last night's wine. "What's gotten into you?"

I shrugged, my mouth too dry to form words.

"You think about your family too much," he sneered. "You think about the farm, about the fields and the sunshine. You're a gladiator now, Dacian. You're property. You're nothing but meat for the games."

I looked up at him, my eyes burning with a fire that had nothing to do with the sun. I thought of my family, of my sister, of the life I'd left behind. I thought of Mars, god of war, of his red right hand, of the oath I'd sworn on my own blood.

"I'll remember," I said, my voice low and deadly. "I'll remember, lanista. I'll make sure of it."

The lanista sneered again, but I saw something in his eyes, something that gave me hope. He saw it too, the spark of defiance, the embers of rebellion that smoldered deep within me. And for a moment, just for a moment, I knew that I was not alone.

I was not just a gladiator, a tool, a piece of meat for the games. I was Dacian, son of Dacia, warrior of Mars. And I would fight, by the gods, I would fight until my dying breath.
